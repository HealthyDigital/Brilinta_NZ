BRILINTA IS DIFFERENT...

BRILINTA is not a thienopyridine1

It is the first oral antiplatelet that acts via P2Y12 with 24 hour systemic exposure to the directly active
compound (when dosed twice daily) and acts via ENT-1, increasing local endogenous adenosine levels*

  • Direct-acting1,2
  • 24-hour systemic exposure2,3
  • Reversible binding
    to the P2Y12 receptor4
  • Acts via both
    P2Y12 and ENT-11-4

*A link between increases in adenosine and clinical outcomes has not been clearly elucidated

RE-UPTAKE VIA THE ENT-1 TRANSPORTER1

This increases the concentration of adenosine in the circulation, and it can

The P2Y12 & ENT-1 pathways mediate both an anti-platelet effect and an enhanced adenosine response effect.
