BRILINTA® (ticagrelor), in combination with aspirin, is indicated for the prevention of atherothrombotic events in adult patients with acute coronary syndromes (ACS), including those managed medically or with invasive techniques.*

Treatment with BRILINTA is recommended for up to 12 months unless discontinuation is clinically indicated.

Contraindications specific to BRILINTA

  • Hypersensitivity to the active substance
    (ticagrelor) or to any of the excipients
  • History of intracranial haemorrhage
  • Moderate-to-severe hepatic impairment
  • Combination with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors, such as ketoconazole, clarithromycin, nefazodone, ritonavir and atazanavir

PRECAUTIONS SPECIFIC TO BRILINTA

The concomitant use of BRILINTA with doses of simvastatin >40mg is not recommended

BRILINTA should be used with caution in patients at risk of bradycardic events

BRILINTA should be used with caution in patients with history of asthma and/or COPD

As a precautionary measure, the use of BRILINTA in patients with uric acid nephropathy is discouraged

Creatinine levels may increase during treatment with BRILINTA. In PLATO, serum creatinine concentration increased by >50% in 8.3% of BRILINTA-treated patients vs. 6.7% of clopidogrel-treated patients. Increases typically did not progress and often decreased with ongoing treatment

Renal function should be checked after 1 month and thereafter according to routine medical practice

*BRILINTA has not been specifically tested in patients
undergoing thrombolysis.
