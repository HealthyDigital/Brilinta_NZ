ATLANTIC is a study that compares ticagrelor pre-hospital vs. ticagrelor in-hospital, evaluating pre-PCI coronary reperfusion1

A 30-day study evaluating the efficacy and safety of pre-hospital vs in-hospital ticagrelor initiation in STEMI patients planned for PCI; median time difference between the loading doses was 31 minutes
Adapted from Montalescot et al. 20131 and Montalescot et al. 20142
*ATLANTIC study protocol; LD, loading dose; R, randomization
Male and female patients aged ≥18 years with documented evidence of acute STEMI were planned for PCI.
Symptom onset was within >30 minutes and <6 hours prior to randomization. Patients had new persistent ST-
segment elevation ≥1mm in two or more contiguous ECG leads. All patients were initially managed by ambulance
personnel; diagnosis and decision to include in the study were made solely by ambulance personnel.1
**Planned N=1,770 (actual N=1,862). Written informed consent was obtained in mobile care unit.

Co-primary endpoints1,2

    CRITERIA FOR TIMI FLOW GRADE 3 IN THE INFARCT-RELATED
    ARTERY AT ANGIOGRAPHY BEFORE PCI1

    •  Early TIMI flow Grade 3 is an independent predictor of survival
        and improved outcomes in patients undergoing primary PCI2

ATLANTIC
secondary endpoints1

  • Composite endpoint of death, MI, stent thrombosis, stroke or urgent revascularisation at 30 days
  • Definite stent thrombosis at 30 days
  • Thrombotic bailout with glycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitors
  • TIMI flow grade 3 at the end of the procedure
  • Complete (≥70%) resolution of ST-segment elevation at 60 minutes after PCI
