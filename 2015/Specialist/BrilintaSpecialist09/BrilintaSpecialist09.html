Adapted from Stone et al. 2011.1 MACE = Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events, CL = Culprit Lesion, NCL = Non-Culprit Lesion.

Some patients had both CL-related and non-CL related events, and some patients had multiple CL related events, multiple
NCL-related events, or both at different times (in which case the first event is represented in the time to event curve).

A prospective study in 697 patients with ACS who underwent three-vessel coronary angiography and gray-scale and radiofrequency intravascular ultrasonographic imaging after PCI. Subsequent MACE (death from cardiac causes, cardiac
arrest, MI or rehospitalisation due to unstable or progressive angina) were adjudicated to be related to either originally
treated (culprit) lesions or untreated (non-culprit) lesions. The median follow-up period was 3.4 years.
