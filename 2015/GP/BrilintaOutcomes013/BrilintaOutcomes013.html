Introducing MyHeart

All patients prescribed Brilinta will be offered the opportunity to enrol in the MyHeart Patient
Support Programme. MyHeart is a free, individualised programme designed by clinical health
psychologists to complement the dialogue you have with your patients by:

  • Supporting adherence to Brilinta
  • Reinforcing positive behaviours including; exercise, attending cardiac
    rehab, smoking cessation and healthy living
  • Nurse Phone Line All patients will have access to a registered nurse
  • Magazines A magazine series designed to inform and support patients
  • Website Displays the content most relevant to the patient
  • SMS Tailored messages of support

dyspnoea – patient care

  • Usually mild to moderate and reported
    early after starting treatment1,2
  • Approximately 30% of dyspnoea events
    resolved within 7 days1
  • The majority of patients reporting
    dyspnoea only experienced a single episode2
  • Dyspnoea generally resolved without
    the need for treatment discontinuation1,2
  • Patients with asthma/COPD may have an
    increased risk of experiencing dyspnoea2
  • Not associated with new or worsening
    heart or lung disease1

Dyspnoea was more common vs. clopidogrel1-3

RISKBENEFIT

BRILINTA-associated dyspnoea was mostly mild-to-moderate in intensity2

Most events were reported as a single episode occurring early after starting treatment2

Dyspnoea was reported as a serious adverse event in 0.14% of patients with BRILINTA
vs. 0.02% with clopidogrel2

Dyspnoea was not associated with new or worsening heart or lung disease2

minor bleeding – patient care

  • Minor bleeding included nose bleeds,
    gum bleeds, bruising and oozing from
    injection sites
  • Patients who discontinue antiplatelet
    therapy prematurely are at increased
    risk for further cardiac events1
  • Seek medical advice before
    discontinuing Brilinta due to minor
    bleeding or other concerns1
  • It would be helpful for your patient to
    inform other physicians and dentists
    that they are taking Brilinta1

Brilinta may be associated with minor bleeding1

PLATO – defined bleeding at 12 months2

  • Defined as any bleeding requiring medical intervention but not meeting the criteria for major bleeding.2
  • Minor bleeding with Brilinta does not generally require discontinuation.2,3

Adapted from Wallentin et al. 20092

Significance was not tested because neither type of bleeding was a pre-specified endpoint in PLATO.2
NB: There was no significant difference in overall major bleeding between Brilinta and clopidogrel.2
There was a significant increase in Non-CABG major and non-procedural major bleeding.

STARTING PATIENTS
ON BRILINTA1

No dose adjustment is required across ACS patient populations including:1

  • age (18+)
  • weight
  • renal impairment
  • mild hepatic impairment
  • CYP2C19 or ABCB1 genotype
  • For patients who are unable to swallow
    the tablets whole, Brilinta 90mg can
    now be crushed to a fine powder and
    mixed in water and drunk immediately.
  • If a patient misses a dose of Brilinta,
    they should take one 90 mg tablet
    (their next dose) at its scheduled time.
