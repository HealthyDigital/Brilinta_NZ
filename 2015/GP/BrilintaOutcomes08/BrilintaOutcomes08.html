The primary efficacy endpoint (a composite of death from vascular causes, MI or stroke at 1 year) was 8.6% for BRILINTA and 11.2% for clopidogrel (ARR 2.2%, RRR 23%; HR 0.77; 95% CI 0.60-0.99) for patients with any loss-of-function allele and 8.8% for BRILINTA and 10.0% for clopidogrel (ARR 1.2%, RRR 14%; HR 0.86; 95% CI 0.74-1.01; interaction p=0.46) for patients with no loss-of-function allele1

Data were consistent in relation to the ABCB1 genotype. Kaplan-Meier estimates of events of the primary outcomes were consistently lower in patients treated with BRILINTA compared with clopidogrel regardless of ABCB1 polymorphisms1
