DIABETES IS A SIGNIFICANT INDEPENDENT RISK
FACTOR FOR MORTALITY IN ACS PATIENTS 1

In a subgroup analysis of 11 independent TIMI study group clinical trials (N=62,036), it was shown that ACS patients with
diabetes (n=10,613,17%) had higher all cause mortality at 30 days and 1 year compared to ACS patients without diabetes.1

PLATO WALLENTIN

REFERENCE:

  1. Donahoe SM et al. JAMA 2007; 298: 765-775.

NOTES

This analysis included 55 countries, 62,036 patients; 46,577 with STEMI, 15,459 with UA/INSTEMI; 17% (10,613) with diabetes.1
Primary endpoint: post-ACS mortality at 30 days and 1 year in patients with or without diabetes.1
DM = Diabetes Mellitus
TIMI = Thrombolysis in Myorcardial Infraction
STEMI = ST-segment Elevation Myocardial Infraction
UA/NSTEMI = Unstable Angine/Non ST-segment Elevation Myorcardial Infraction
